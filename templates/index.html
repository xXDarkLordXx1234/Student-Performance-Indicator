Project Architecture

End-to-End ML Pipeline

Built with a modular, production-ready structure — each stage is a separate component with logging, exception handling, and artifact persistence.

1
Data Ingestion
Reads raw CSV, performs 80/20 train-test split, saves artifacts to disk
2
Data Transformation
StandardScaler for numerics, OneHotEncoder + imputation for categoricals via ColumnTransformer
3
Model Training
GridSearchCV tunes 9 models; best model by R² is saved as model.pkl
4
Predict Pipeline
Loads preprocessor.pkl and model.pkl to transform and score new inputs at inference time
5
Flask Web App
REST API with a form UI — submits student data and returns a predicted math score
Exploratory Data Analysis

What the Data Reveals

Before modelling, a thorough EDA was conducted across all variables — uncovering the strongest predictors of student performance.

ANALYSIS 01
Gender vs. Performance
Histograms and boxplots split by gender reveal females consistently outscore males across all parental education levels and lead in overall pass percentage.
ANALYSIS 02
Parental Education Impact
Students with parents holding a master's or bachelor's degree score significantly higher. Higher education lifts the floor — the bottom 25% improve the most.
ANALYSIS 03
Race & Ethnicity Groups
Group E students achieved the highest average scores across all subjects. Group A scored the lowest, reflecting socioeconomic patterns in academic outcomes.
ANALYSIS 04
Lunch Type as a Proxy
Students on standard lunch consistently outperform those on free/reduced lunch — a strong socioeconomic proxy and one of the top predictors in the final model.
ANALYSIS 05
Test Preparation Course
Students who completed the test prep course scored higher across all three subjects — math, reading, and writing — compared to those who skipped it.
ANALYSIS 06
Score Correlations
Pairplot analysis confirmed that math, reading, and writing scores increase linearly with each other — strong multicollinearity that benefits linear regression models.
Females lead in pass percentage and are top scorers across all parental education categories
Master's degree group shows the widest gender gap — female median is notably higher than male in this group
High school parent group has the most inconsistent scores — widest IQR and most spread in both genders
All three scores increase linearly — a student who reads well also writes well and performs better in maths
Parental education raises the floor, not just the ceiling — Q1 scores improve most with higher parental education
Zero missing values in the dataset — no imputation required for EDA; clean data throughout
